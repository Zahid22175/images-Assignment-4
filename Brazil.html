Brazil

Brazil is the largest country in South America, known for its vibrant culture, diverse landscapes, and stunning natural wonders. The official language is Portuguese, and the currency is the Brazilian Real.

Christ the Redeemer - Key Points:

    • Location: Christ the Redeemer is located in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, atop the Corcovado Mountain.

    • Height: The statue stands 98 feet (30 meters) tall, with arms stretching 92 feet (28 meters) wide.

    • Construction: The statue was completed in 1931 after nearly 9 years of construction, and it was designed by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa.

    • Material: It is made of reinforced concrete and covered with soapstone to protect it from the elements.

    • Symbolism: Christ the Redeemer symbolizes Christianity and is a symbol of peace, welcoming all who visit Rio de Janeiro.

    • UNESCO World Heritage: The statue is part of the "Cultural Landscape of Rio de Janeiro" UNESCO World Heritage site.

    • Lighting: The statue is lit up at night, making it a striking sight for tourists and locals alike.

    • Tourist Attraction: It is one of the most visited landmarks in Brazil, attracting millions of visitors every year.

    • Renovations: The statue underwent significant renovations in recent years to maintain its structural integrity, including restoring the outer stone covering.

Unique Fact: Christ the Redeemer is one of the most photographed monuments in the world, offering an unforgettable view of Rio de Janeiro and the surrounding coastline.
